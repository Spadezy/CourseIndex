Reading Discussion II: My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge...

1. From what I understand and think, Lialina's definition of the universal user is pretty clear and accurate. Lialina describes universal users as people who are being independent and doing things in their own way. The definition helps make Schwulst's argument for individual websites much stronger over platforms. Many platforms in the present day don't have much security, which is why many people cannot trust many online sources to do their actions. However, I also like to see universal users as just normal ordinary users because of how the vast internet is actually divided. By this I mean, many categories have their own websites for their different users.

For example, there are websites for music like Spotify, there are websites for shopping like Amazon, or there are websites to display images like Instagram and other social media platforms. There are many websites for all kinds of things, although to do many of these things at once, you'd need to have all these websites open up at the same time. Schwulst mentions the ideas of a personal website, where you can do all the things you want, on one singular website instead of having many things open. Having anything open on one website in your organized fashion makes things much easier and simpler. On top of that, personal websites have much better security in comparison of having 3 public websites open at the same time.
